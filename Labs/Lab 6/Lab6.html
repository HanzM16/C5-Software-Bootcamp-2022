What exactly is a favicon?

A favicon is a pixel icon that represents your website's branding. Its goal is to make it easy for visitors to find your page. Due to their small size, website icons perform best when images with a few text characters. Favicons are not to be confused with logos, though they are often used equally. A favicon can also be a little small icon that represents a website's branding. Its goal is to make it easy for visitors to find a particular page. Favicons are not to be confused with logos, however they are sometimes used interchangeably.

How are favicons created?

A favicon image can be created with a program like Fireworks, Photoshop, Corel Paint, or a free open-source alternative. Many organizations simply use their company logos to maintain a consistent brand identity. Start with any size, but a 64 x 64 pixel square is easier to edit, and then you can reduce it to 16 x 16 pixels if you want. After that, save the file as .jpg, .png, .gif, .bmp, or .tif. Once you're satisfied with the image, save it as favicon.ico. Most web browsers support this format as the recommended one. You may need to use an internet application like iconifier.net, prodraw.net, or faviconer.com to convert your file to the.ico format. There are many options to choose from. You can then download your fresh new.ico file after uploading your image and selecting the icon size. The.ico file should be placed in your website's root directory. When you visit the website, the image should appear. You may then change your website's HTML page and include HTML code to help older browsers find your favicon image.
